Immersion & Research

I designed and conducted an online quantitative study using a survey completed by 122 participants (ages 30‚Äì50, predominantly women). The goal was to gather data-driven insights from both interested and non-interested users to better understand their needs, perceived necessity, and expectations around existing natural health solutions.

This approach allowed me to identify key pain points and opportunities related to how users engage with and perceive current content and offerings in the natural health space.

Research image

Key Findings

Quality of Life as the Main Driver

Users primarily seek to improve their quality of life (78%) and prefer Google, their personal network, and social media for information.

Design a guided experience that helps users identify suitable natural solutions aligned with their lifestyle goals.

Trust Built on Evidence and Experience

Trust is conditional on scientific evidence (74.8%) and personal experience (62.9%).

Include verified expert content and community feedback to strengthen credibility.

Overwhelmed and Underserved

Main pain points include information overload with contradictory sources, lack of personalization, and difficulty evaluating the real effectiveness of proposed solutions.

Build a personalized recommendation system or filtering tool to simplify decision-making.

Based on these findings, I created 3 personas representing target profiles (preventive user, user with chronic needs, parent), mapped user journeys to identify friction points, and prioritized user stories to guide feature development.

This research validated user needs and oriented design decisions toward evidence-based content, simplified navigation, and increased personalization.

Identity & Branding

Multiple graphic proposals were merged to finalize a cohesive brand, including color-coded pillars, mascot design, visual style, and illustrations.

To support this shift, a new visual identity was developed, centered around a mascot: the pomegranate, symbolizing health benefits such as supporting blood pressure and circulation through polyphenols, strengthening the immune system via vitamin C, and promoting healthy digestion with natural enzymes.

Mascots foster emotional connection, enhance memorability, and differentiate the brand while conveying scientific authority in a friendly, approachable way.

With the visual identity and case study finalized, I moved on to wireframing and high-fidelity prototyping. To ensure efficiency, I structured and configured reusable components, allowing quick iteration and consistent design updates across screens.

Iterative Improvements & Testing

After returning in July 2025 for one month, I conducted a heuristic audit assessing interface efficiency and user experience, an accessibility review ensuring compliance with standards for readability and usability, and a content review optimizing format and clarity for desktop users.

Subsequent improvements focused on refining the desktop experience while maintaining the playful, lifestyle-oriented identity of the app.
